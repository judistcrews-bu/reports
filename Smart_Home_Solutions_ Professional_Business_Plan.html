Executive Summary

Business Concept

A boutique home automation consultancy specializing in open-source solutions (Home Assistant) and custom integrations that go beyond typical "out-of-the-box" smart home products. Targeting homeowners in the Knoxville, Tennessee area who want sophisticated, personalized automation without being locked into proprietary ecosystems.

Unique Value Proposition

Four decades of IT architecture experience applied to residential automation, with expertise in:

  • Custom Home Assistant dashboards and integrations
  • Advanced media server automation (Plex "NOW PLAYING" displays)
  • Synchronized holiday lighting displays with xLights
  • Custom home theater automation
  • Solutions that mainstream installers can't or won't provide

Business Model

Part-time consulting service with carefully limited capacity to avoid conflicts with full-time employment at BetterUp. Initial focus on proof-of-concept projects to validate market demand before scaling.

Target Market

Tech-curious homeowners in the greater Knoxville area who are frustrated with the limitations of consumer smart home products and want customized solutions.

Market Opportunity

Target Customer Segments

Primary: "Frustrated Adopters"

Profile: Homeowners who bought Ring/Nest/Alexa but hit limitations and want integrations that don't exist in consumer apps.

Pain Points: Devices don't talk to each other, limited automation options, locked into specific ecosystems

Willingness to Pay: High - willing to invest $2,000-5,000 for customization

Local Market Size: Estimated 5,000-10,000 households in greater Knoxville

Secondary: "Home Theater Enthusiasts"

Profile: Already have Plex/media servers but basic automation. Want impressive custom displays and theater experiences.

Showcase Example: Your Dakboard "NOW PLAYING" integration is a perfect demonstration

Characteristics: High engagement, willing to invest in unique experiences, value craftsmanship

Tertiary: "Holiday Display Enthusiasts"

Profile: Currently doing manual light shows or basic timers. Want xLights/synchronized displays but lack technical knowledge.

Technology: Raspberry Pi-based controllers and custom sequences

Seasonality: Strong revenue opportunity October-January, potential for ongoing maintenance contracts

Market Validation Strategy

Before investing heavily, test demand through:

  • 3-5 pilot projects at discounted rates (30% off)
  • Document before/after showcases for portfolio
  • Collect testimonials and referrals
  • Assess time investment vs. compensation
  • Identify most profitable project types

Risk Management

Key Risks and Mitigation

Risk: Time Conflict with BetterUp

Mitigation: Strict 15-hour weekly limit, no same-day service calls, clear client expectations about availability

Action Item: Review BetterUp employee handbook regarding outside consulting work

Risk: Technical Support Burden

Mitigation: Document everything, create system diagrams, train clients thoroughly, 90-day warranty only

Boundary Setting: No 24/7 support, scheduled check-ins only

Risk: Scope Creep

Mitigation: Detailed SOW for every project, change order process, fixed-price contracts with clear deliverables

Risk: Liability (Electrical/Property Damage)

Mitigation: General liability insurance ($1M coverage), licensed electrician for any electrical work, clear disclaimers

Risk: Market Demand Uncertainty

Mitigation: Start with pilot projects, minimal upfront investment, pivot services based on demand signals

Legal/Compliance Considerations

Business Structure: Start as Sole Proprietor (simplest)

  • Consider LLC in Year 2 if revenue exceeds $50k
  • Track expenses meticulously for tax deductions
  • Quarterly estimated tax payments if profitable

Contracts: Use simple service agreements

  • Define scope, deliverables, timelines
  • Payment terms (50% upfront, 50% on completion)
  • Warranty limitations
  • Liability disclaimers

Licensing: Most smart home work doesn't require contractor licenses

  • Confirm Tennessee requirements
  • Partner with licensed electrician if needed
  • No HVAC work (requires license)

Go/No-Go Decision Criteria

After 3 Pilot Projects, Evaluate:

✓ Go Forward If:

  • Projects completed within time estimates
  • Clients enthusiastic and willing to refer
  • Enjoying the work and not feeling burned out
  • Profit margin >60% after all costs
  • No conflicts with BetterUp role

⚠ Pause/Pivot If:

  • Projects taking 50%+ longer than estimated
  • Client acquisition cost too high
  • Difficulty balancing with full-time job
  • Low referral rates (<30%)
  • Technical support burden too heavy

✗ Stop If:

  • BetterUp raises concerns about conflict
  • Personal stress/health impact
  • Consistent negative client feedback
  • Unprofitable after adjustments
  • Market demand lower than expected
